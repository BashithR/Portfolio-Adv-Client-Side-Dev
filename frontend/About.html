My Education

  • Primary Education

here's a story that will inspire you...

Back in primary school, I wasn’t much of an athlete. I wasn’t a top student either. I was just trying to keep up—struggling with my studies, hoping to improve. I didn’t stand out, and honestly, I didn’t know if I ever would.

But I had one thing that made a difference: my best friend. He was the class topper—smart, kind, and someone who always seemed to know what to do. He didn’t just help me with schoolwork; he believed in me. He called me his best friend, and that meant something. We stuck together through everything—good days, bad days, exams, failures. That friendship was real.

Little by little, I started catching up. People noticed. I moved up in class rankings, eventually becoming third. He was still first, but I was right there behind him. For the first time, I felt like I was doing something right.

Then he left the school—

Before he left, he told me, “You’ll take my place.” I didn’t know if I could—but I tried. And I did. I became first in class. That moment wasn’t just about being the best—it was about proving to myself that I could be good at something. That I mattered.

What I Learned...

Growth isn’t about being perfect—it’s about being persistent.

Friendship can be the most powerful form of mentorship.

Belief—whether it’s yours or someone else’s—can push you forward.

Success feels sweeter when it’s shared.

The Message I Carry Forward...

I’m not sharing this to brag. I’m sharing it because someone out there might feel the way I did—average, stuck, unsure. If that’s you, find people who lift you up. Stick with them. Let them help you grow. And when it’s your turn, do the same for someone else.

"I wasn’t born special... I became better because someone believed I could be..."
